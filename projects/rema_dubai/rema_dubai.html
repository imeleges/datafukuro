Brief description

The REMA Dubai project is an exploratory data analysis of the residential rental real estate market in Dubai, specifically in the Downtown and DIFC areas. The data, which was collected from the Bayut website, offers insights into apartment pricing, area, and features.

Description

The project analyzes over 4800 apartment listings from August 2022, focusing on parameters such as rental price, address, number of bedrooms, area in sq.ft., amenities, and others. New features were added for more comprehensive analysis, including the division of the address and date into more specific components, and the creation of a "price per square foot" measure.

Analysis of the listings revealed a wide range in prices and areas.

Most listings were between 90,000 and 300,000 AED, with a notable spike in one-bedroom apartments at around 100,000 AED. Areas ranged from 37 to 6,792 sq.ft., with a median of 1,346 sq.ft. The most listed options were 2-bedroom apartments, followed by 1-bedroom and then 3-bedroom apartments.

Further analysis of 1 and 2 bedroom apartments revealed common features that could potentially affect prices, such as being fully furnished, having a fitted kitchen, or including bills.

This project provides valuable insights for individuals or businesses interested in the Dubai rental market. It can help users understand pricing trends and the most common apartment features in Downtown and DIFC areas. It's especially useful for newcomers to Dubai to better understand the factors that influence rental prices and for those interested in 1 or 2 bedroom apartments. The project's methodology of cleaning, categorizing, and analyzing data could also be applied to other markets or datasets for similar insights.

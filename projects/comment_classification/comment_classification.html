Brief description

The project focuses on creating a tool for the online store to detect toxic comments and send them for moderation. As users can edit and supplement product descriptions, the need arises to classify user comments into positive or negative categories.

Description

The online store is introducing a new service where users can actively participate by editing product descriptions, like in wiki communities. Users can propose their edits and leave comments on the changes made by other users. Given this interactive setup, the chance of negative or hurtful comments increases.

The project's primary objective is to design a model that can classify these comments effectively. The dataset provided has labels that specify the toxicity of comments. The goal is for the model to achieve an F1 score of at least 0.75, ensuring reliable comment classification.

The process involved loading and preparing the data, training multiple models, testing the successful ones. And in conclusion multiple models were tested to achieve a balanced precision and recall, with the primary aim of attaining an F1 score of 0.75 or higher.

Brief description

The taxi company is leveraging historical data on taxi orders at airports. The objective is to create a model capable of predicting the number of taxi orders for the forthcoming hour. The model's performance is evaluated based on the RMSE metric, aiming for a value no greater than 48 on the test set.

Description

Data Preparation: The project starts with the reading of the data file and transforming the datetime information. The dataset comprises two columns: datetime and num_orders. The data is then resampled hourly.

Data Analysis: A thorough analysis of the data is performed. The number of taxi orders demonstrates a daily seasonality pattern. Taxi orders peak at midnight and dip at 6 a.m. This pattern might be influenced by the limited availability of public transportation during late hours.

Model Training and Testing: New features like calendar attributes, lagged values, and rolling mean are introduced in the dataset. Multiple models including Linear Regression, Decision Tree Regressor, and Random Forest Regressor are trained and evaluated based on the RMSE metric.

At the end of the research, several models were evaluated. The results are summarized in the table:

The models DecisionTreeRegressor and RandomForestRegressor showed satisfactory prediction quality.

However, the RandomForestRegressor appeared as the better model for both the training and test datasets. This was achieved after hyperparameter tuning. The final model is a RandomForestRegressor with hyperparameters n_estimators = 95 and max_depth = 19, resulting in an RMSE = 43.80042268131265.

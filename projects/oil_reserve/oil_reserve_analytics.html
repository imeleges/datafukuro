Brief description

In this project, machine learning and statistical analysis are used to identify the optimal region for the development of new oil wells, based on potential profit and risk assessments. This analysis provides a robust foundation for strategic decision-making in oil extraction.

Description

The initial phase of the project involved data collection from three different regions, focusing on the characteristics of oil samples from wells, such as the quality and volume of oil reserves. Using this information, a machine learning model employing linear regression was trained to predict the volume of reserves in new wells, helping to identify the regions with the highest potential total profit.

The potential profit and associated risks were assessed using bootstrap techniques, providing a comprehensive understanding of the potential outcomes from developing oil wells in each region.

Following the analysis of data from the three regions, the training of linear regression models, and the evaluation of potential profits and risks, a specific region was chosen for the development of the oil field. This region yielded the highest average revenue per well and had only a 1% chance of incurring a loss, indicating a favorable return on investment.

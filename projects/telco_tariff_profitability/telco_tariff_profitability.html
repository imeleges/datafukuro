Evaluating Telco Tariff Profitability

Project details
Project Github Repository
January 2022
Tariff analysis, User behavior analysis, Descriptive statistics, Statistical hypothesis testing, Python, Pandas, Matplotlib, SciPy, Seaborn
Brief description

The project aims to analyze two tariff plans - 'Smart' and 'Ultra', to help the commercial department of the cellular service provider determine which tariff ('plan' or 'package') is more profitable.

Description

Initial study of the tariffs on a small sample of 500 users from the year 2018. The sample provided details like who they are, where they're from, what tariff they use, and how many calls and messages each one sent. The company "Megaline" rounds up the values of minutes and megabytes. If a user spoke for even a second, a full minute is counted. Data from these tariffs were processed according to these rules.

In the case of the "Smart" tariff, many users use almost the entire package of minutes and several exceed the limit. "Ultra" users on average use more minutes, but none of them exhaust the high limit set by the tariff. Similar patterns were observed with the usage of messages and internet traffic.

Upon analyzing the data, it was found that more than half of the "Smart" tariff users exceeded their tariff limits, purchasing additional minutes, messages, or internet traffic. However, "Ultra" tariff users usually stayed within their limit, with rare purchases of additional resources.

The average revenue per user per month was 1230 rubles for the "Smart" tariff and 2060 rubles for the "Ultra" tariff, suggesting that the company makes more on average from "Ultra" users. Although the average revenue from users in Moscow was slightly higher than other cities, the difference was not statistically significant.
